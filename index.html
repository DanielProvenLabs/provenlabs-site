Real Estate Agencies & Brokerages

Most brokerages are losing deals they already won — not from bad agents, but from broken follow-up systems.

  • $241B industry — the biggest player holds only 9% market share
  • 48% of agents never follow up; 60% of buyers say no 4 times before saying yes
  • Top earners get 70% of business from referrals and repeat clients — most have no system for it
  • Tech overload is the real problem, not lack of tools
  • 240,000+ small independent brokerages underserved and ready

Full Research Profile

  • Market sizing: $206B in 2026 → $281B by 2031 at 5.32% CAGR. Residential brokerage delivers 82.4% of revenue.
  • The NAR settlement fallout — flat-fee and rebate models accelerating, new disclosure requirements, fair housing updates increasing liability exposure.
  • Software landscape: Follow Up Boss, Wise Agent, Lofty, BoldTrail, CINC. Adoption and simplicity are the real bottleneck, not the existence of software.
  • The lock-in effect — existing home sales near 30-year lows (4.06M transactions in 2026) as homeowners hold sub-4% loans.
  • 81% of brokerage firms operate a single office; average firm has just 2 full-time licensees.
  • Broker isolation problem: agents have reported communicating with their broker as few as 10 times in 13 years.
⚖️

Law Firms

The average small firm loses over $100,000 a year in billable time to admin work. That's not a staffing problem — it's a systems problem.

  • $405B industry — 77% of legal problems never reach a professional
  • 60% of law firms never answer email inquiries; 27% don't return phone calls
  • 78% have CRM software, only 7% actually use it
  • 45% of small firm time spent on admin — $129,600 in lost revenue per attorney per year
  • Client intake is broken — leads die at first contact through manual back-and-forth and data entry
  • 150,000–200,000 small and solo firms underserved and ready

Full Research Profile

  • Market: $405.3B in 2026, growing at 2.4% CAGR. Small firm segment (2–10 attorneys) holds 34.2% market share, fastest growing at 5.9% CAGR.
  • Software landscape: Clio (used by 150,000+ legal professionals), MyCase, PracticePanther, Lawmatics, Smokeball, Filevine. Adoption is the bottleneck.
  • Billing leakage — firms only collect 89–90% of what they bill, leaving 10% of revenue on the table.
  • AI adoption: 26% of firms integrating generative AI (up from 14% in 2024), but small firms lag due to resource constraints and ethics concerns.
  • Lawyers lose 40% of working time to inefficient manual processes outside the practice of law.
  • The ethics/confidentiality barrier — AI introduction must be trust-first and ethics-aware to win over cautious small firm owners.

Automotive Repair Shops & Dealerships

Two-thirds of Americans don't trust their repair shop. The ones winning right now are the ones who fixed their customer experience first.

  • $199B market growing to $281B by 2031 at 5.9% CAGR
  • Two-thirds of Americans don't trust auto repair shops — a credibility gap hiding an opportunity
  • Most shops have no structured system to bring customers back — retention is entirely reactive
  • Job cards lost, invoicing manual, vehicle history on paper — missed billable work every day
  • 115,000+ independent owner-operated shops on legacy tools or nothing at all

Full Research Profile

  • Market: $199B in 2026, 162,000 establishments. The 50 largest companies generate less than 10% of revenue — majority comes from small independents.
  • Average US vehicle age reached 12.8 years in 2026 — older vehicles drive more frequent repairs and higher ticket sizes.
  • Software landscape: Tekmetric (modern, cloud-based, best for independents), Mitchell 1, RO Writer, Shop-Ware, AutoLeap. Dealerships use CDK Global, Reynolds & Reynolds.
  • Technician shortage: 46% of shop owners say it's significantly impacting operations.
  • Parts pricing pressure: 46% cite finding affordable parts and rising prices as their top challenge.
  • Dealership threat: 16.8% of shops say competing with larger players is getting harder.

Contractors & Construction

Most contractors are leaving 10–15% of their revenue on the table every year — not from bad work, but from no system to collect it.

  • $2.2 trillion market — 3.7 million businesses, the vast majority running lean with no real systems
  • Poor communication causes 52% of rework — costing the industry $31.3 billion annually
  • 70% of contractors experience payment delays beyond 30 days
  • Only 18% use anything beyond email or spreadsheets
  • 600,000+ small owner-operated contractors with everything living in the owner's head

Full Research Profile

  • Market: $2.2 trillion in 2026, 4.4% of US GDP, 8.3M people employed. Residential construction rebounding 12%; non-residential up 8% in 2026.
  • Software landscape: Procore (enterprise), Buildertrend (residential), JobTread (fastest growing for small contractors at $199/mo), ServiceTitan. Only 18% use apps beyond email or spreadsheets.
  • Labor crisis: industry needs 439,000 new workers in 2026 alone; 62% of firms say candidates lack required skills.
  • 73% report that using multiple disconnected tools makes data sharing harder — yet nearly three-quarters plan to increase software spending in 2026.
  • Material cost pressure: construction input prices rose at a 6% annualized rate through mid-2026.
  • Most contractors are tradespeople first, business owners second — software adoption must be frictionless and ROI must be obvious.

Accounting & Bookkeeping Firms

The firms growing right now aren't working harder — they cut the admin and went all in on advisory. AI made that possible.

  • $145.5B industry — 886,000 firms, most running 8 disconnected apps with none talking to each other
  • Firms can serve 50% more clients with the same staff when AI automation is in place
  • 79% expect AI to have a high or transformational impact — only 14% have a defined strategy
  • Two-thirds feel overwhelmed by technology complexity at least weekly
  • No formal marketing, no outbound — growth is entirely word of mouth or it doesn't happen

Full Research Profile

  • Market: $145.5B in 2026. AI accounting market projected to reach $10.87B in 2026, growing at a 44.6% CAGR. SME adoption is the primary driver.
  • Software landscape: QuickBooks (dominant), Xero, FreshBooks, Karbon, Financial Cents, Botkeeper, Dext. On average firms use 8 different apps for core operations.
  • The eight-app problem: 44% cite subscription costs, 41% integration difficulties, 41% time-consuming data entry as major issues.
  • The advisory opportunity: 80% of accountants expect strategic advisory to grow 38% in the next year — but advisory knowledge lives in senior practitioners' heads and can't scale without systems.
  • Talent crisis: aging CPA population retiring, fewer entering the profession, mounting compliance workloads stretching thin teams.
  • AI adoption barriers: privacy/security risk (41%), lack of staff experience (39%), data quality issues (38%).
